# Accessibility Research Notes

## Questions To Answer For Accessibility For All Users

### 1) What helps a user learn more in an environment?

- Reading
- Watching videos
- Listening to explanations
- Allowing a user to try and learn through their own mistakes

### 2) What helps users recognise information in a good way?

- Written down
- Shown with pictures
- Explained out loud
- Step-by-step demonstrations created through work made by helpful resources

### 3) What do most users do when learning?

- Highlight or reread notes
- Draw diagrams or mind maps
- Read notes aloud or discuss them
- Practice questions or activities (main)

### 4) For users with accessibility needs, how long is focus time?

- Less than 10 minutes
- 10-20 minutes
- 20-40 minutes
- More than 40 minutes

### 5) For accessibility needs, are paragraphs hard to read?

- Never
- Sometimes
- Often
- Almost always
- All specific needs for different users

### 6) Can background noise be too distracting?

- Never
- Sometimes
- Often
- Always
- All specific needs for different users

### 7) Which type of learning do you enjoy the most?

- Reading articles or books
- Watching animations or videos
- Listening to podcasts or teachers
- Building, experimenting, or solving problems

### 8) What helps with active recall in the moment?

- Reading it again
- Seeing a picture or diagram
- Hearing someone explain it
- Doing it again

### 9) Are timed tasks effective for different users?

- I like them
- They're okay
- I find them stressful
- I need extra time

### 10) What would make learning easier for users?

- Larger text
- More pictures
- Audio support
- Shorter lessons
- More practice activities
- Less distractions

## Learning Profiles

| Learning Profile     | Description                                          | Best Revision Methods                                                                  |
| -------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Reader               | Learns best by reading and writing notes.            | Cornell notes, summaries, blurting, active recall, practice questions                  |
| Visual Learner       | Understands information through images and diagrams. | Mind maps, diagrams, colour coding, flowcharts, videos, infographics                   |
| Audio Learner        | Remembers information by listening and speaking.     | Text-to-speech, podcasts, recording notes, teaching someone else, verbal active recall |
| Hands-On Learner     | Learns by doing and practising.                      | Practical activities, experiments, quizzes, simulations, exam questions                |
| Focused Sprinter     | Concentrates better in short bursts.                 | 15-25 minute Pomodoro sessions, flashcards, mini quizzes, spaced repetition            |
| Step-by-Step Learner | Prefers information broken into small chunks.        | Chunked notes, checklists, flashcards, scaffolded questions, one topic at a time       |
| Challenge Seeker     | Enjoys testing themselves.                           | Timed exam questions, active recall, blurting, mock exams, quizzes                     |
| Balanced Learner     | Doesn't strongly prefer one method.                  | Mix of flashcards, videos, active recall, mind maps, and practice questions            |
